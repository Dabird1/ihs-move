What is MOVE?

Four elements. Each owned differently at each altitude of the organization.
M
What Is Happening

Meaning

Define three essential components: what we are changing, what will be lost, and what we will gain. Loss cannot be buried — it must be named explicitly before the change advances.

O
Who Will Make It Happen

Ownership

Identify who is responsible at each altitude for turning Meaning into reality. Ownership is altitude-based, not a RACI. Every level has a different, non-delegable job in the change.

V
How Will It Happen

Velocity

Define end goals and reverse-engineer the milestones, benchmarks, messaging, tasks, and support required. Velocity is coaching and obstacle removal — not just a project timeline.

E
How We Know It Happened

Establishment

Define how the change is verified and solidified as the new standard. Before launch, know what metric, document, and performance mechanism locks the change in place.

✕  MOVE is NOT
  • A communication plan
  • A project management tool
  • A RACI chart
  • A top-down enterprise-only process
  • Something you complete and file away
✓  MOVE IS
  • A discipline for every level of the org
  • A living document owned by a Change Champion
  • A framework that makes loss visible and explicit
  • A stage-gated standard before cascading
  • A record of how change happened

The Four Elements

Plays, standards, and what the Change Champion owns at each stage
M

Meaning

What Is Happening — owned by the Change Champion
The Standard

Write all three components. All of them. Not just the first one. What is changing. What it will cost (loss). What are the possibilities. The play is not complete until all three are defined.

1

Meaning Construction / Alignment

The Change Champion defines what needs to change, an understanding of what will be lost and who will pay the cost, and the perceived benefits. The play is not complete until all three components are defined. This must also be accompanied by defining Establishment — what will be measured and how to ensure the change occurred.

2

The Loss Investigation Loop

Before any further step, consult at least two individuals who will "pay the cost." Name them, then capture the additional losses they surface so they can be folded into the Meaning without changing its direction. Silence on a changed fact is spin.

3

Meaning Empathetic Evaluation

Step into the paying individual's perspective. Dissect the three-part Meaning at each level — their loss, their gains, the impact on daily work. This is an empathetic exercise, not a checkbox.

4

Evolving Meaning

Accept that facts may require Meaning to be updated. Log what changed, when, and how you communicated it explicitly. All stakeholders align — silence on a changed fact is not acceptable.

O

Ownership

Who Will Make It Happen — owned by the Change Champion
The Standard

Identify who is responsible for turning Meaning into reality. Ownership is altitude-based — not a RACI. Every level has a different job. Selective translation rather than holistic adoption is failure.

1

Ownership Assignment

The Change Champion and key stakeholders identify who will be responsible for messaging, tasks, and results. These will differ with each change and at various levels of the organization.

2

Ownership Loop

Before any further step, individuals who will own facets of the change are notified and given an opportunity to provide feedback before Velocity is determined. In instances of disconnect or refusal, Meaning is re-evaluated. If an owner cannot fulfill assigned responsibilities, a new owner is selected.

V

Velocity

How Will It Happen — owned by the Change Champion
The Standard

Work with Owners of Meaning to ensure all tasks and results have clear dates, benchmarks, and milestones. Velocity is the implementation phase — cascading through owners to ultimate end users. The real work is coaching, obstacle removal, resistance diagnosis, and making trade-offs clear.

1

Executing the Tasks to Adopt the Change

The Change Champion and owners define timelines with clear milestones (Progress Check-ins) and benchmarks (Standards Check-ins). Owners complete their items at the agreed standards, including communication of Meaning, training for new behaviors, and assessments of adoption.

2

Development Needs Assessment

If a development need is identified during Velocity, owners are responsible for escalating to the appropriate resource. Development needs are incorporated and addressed in parallel — Velocity does not pause.

3

Resistance Recognition and Response

Resistance is diagnostic data. The Change Champion identifies patterns, traces resistance to its source — Meaning misalignment, loss not acknowledged, ownership gap — and responds with targeted intervention.

E

Establishment

How We Know It Happened — owned by the Change Champion
The Standard

The change has taken place and is the new normal. Before launch, know what metric changes, what document changes, what performance or recognition mechanism changes, and what recurring agenda item keeps the change alive.

1

Audit Against Established Standards

Owners conduct an audit of their results and document current status in comparison to the goals and standards decided during initial change discussions.

2

60-Day Establishment Audit

A structured review of every system that still measures, encourages, or rewards the old behavior. Systems that reinforce the past must be identified and updated before Establishment is declared complete.

3

Internal Language Updated at 90 Days

Scan of official documents, templates, job descriptions, onboarding materials, and recurring meetings. New employees have no awareness of the prior reality.

Stage Gates — Go / No-Go

Four decisions before cascading
These are go/no-go gates. A Change Champion who cascades without meeting these standards is not behind on preparation — they are cascading an incomplete change. Check each gate before moving forward.
1
✓

Have we defined MEANING?

Standard

The three components — What is Changing, What will be Lost, and What are the Possibilities — are clear. Readers will know what problem the change is intended to solve.

2
✓

Have we enlisted OWNERSHIP?

Standard

All aspects of the change have defined owners who have been enlisted, accepted their responsibilities, and are prepared to execute on necessary actions.

3
✓

Have we defined VELOCITY?

Standard

A clear roadmap exists with defined milestones, benchmarks, a communication plan, and owned execution paths. The path to adoption is documented and visible to all owners.

4
✓

Have we defined ESTABLISHMENT?

Standard

The locking mechanisms are identified: which metric changes, which document changes, which performance mechanism changes, and what recurring agenda item keeps the change alive.

0 of 4 gates cleared — not ready to cascade

Altitude Architecture

Enterprise change requires all levels. No altitude can do another's work — and none of the work disappears when it is skipped. It surfaces later, at a higher cost.
ELT
Executive Leadership Team
Construct Meaning. Determine Ownership. Define Velocity. Install Establishment measures. The change begins and ends here.
Cascading a change without all three Meaning components defined.
Senior Leadership
Senior Leadership (Org Oversight)
Understand and amplify Meaning — their belief is the precondition for Brand President belief. Co-Own both Velocity and Establishment.
Selective translation rather than holistic adoption. Forwarding the message without translating it to their context.
Brand Support Center
Brand Support Center
Simultaneously Owners and Subjects of change. Model Meaning personally and execute Velocity — they are giving up or adding function. BSC is not exempt.
Treating change as something that happens to others, not to BSC itself.
Regional VP
Regional Vice Presidents
Translate Meaning into regional context. Own Velocity within their geography. Surface resistance as diagnostic data up the chain.
Delegating Meaning translation entirely to Brand Presidents without providing regional context.
Brand Presidents
Brand Presidents
Translate Meaning into brand-specific context. Own Establishment at the brand level. Define what the change means for the frontline in their brand.
Compliance signaling without genuine adoption. Completing the Roadmap without internalizing it.
Frontline
Frontline Leaders & Teams
Adopt the new behavior. Provide honest feedback on what is and isn't working. Are entitled to understand the Why — not just the What.
Being asked to change behavior without being given Meaning — producing compliance, not commitment.
